How weather affects bat detections
🌡️ Best TemperatureiBest Temperature — the average evening temperature on nights when bats were detected, weighted by how many detections each night had. Calculated by matching each active bat night to that night's maximum temperature from the garden weather station, so warmer, busier nights count for more. Bats are generally inactive below about 10°C.
—
Most active above this
🌧️ Rain ImpactiRain Impact — compares the average number of detections on dry nights versus wet nights (more than 1mm of rain). Each bat night is matched to that night's rainfall from the weather station. Bats hunt far less in rain, as it hampers their echolocation and grounds the insects they feed on.
—
Detections on dry vs wet nights
🌙 Moon ImpactiMoon Impact — compares average detections on dark nights (moon less than 30% lit) versus bright, moonlit nights. Each night's moon illumination is calculated from its date. Some bats show "lunar phobia" — avoiding bright nights when they're more visible to predators — though local factors like temperature can mask the effect.
